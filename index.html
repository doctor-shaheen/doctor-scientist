My Research Story

Bridging clinical understanding with scientific discovery.

From foundational wet-lab science...

...to leading large-scale international collaborations...

...to building the future of AI-driven medical research.

My journey in research began early in medical school, fueled by a desire to bridge clinical understanding with scientific discovery. I gained foundational hands-on experience in basic science, working as a research assistant in an animal physiology lab where I learned rodent handling, behavioral experiments, brain dissection, and even introductory stereotactic surgery techniques and basic molecular biology methods like Western Blot. This provided me with a crucial understanding of experimental design and wet-lab work, culminating in my first publications.

Phase 1: Foundational Experience

From Wet Lab to Clinical Data

  • Hands-on experience in animal modeling, stereotactic surgery, and cell culture techniques.
  • Worked as a Clinical Research Assistant in Rheumatology and Neuropsychiatry.
  • Gained crucial understanding of experimental design, clinical study protocols, and patient data.
As I progressed through medical training, my interest expanded to clinical research. I worked as a clinical research assistant, gaining exposure to patient data and study protocols. Simultaneously, I began developing strong quantitative skills, focusing on biostatistics and research methodology, which I later taught to fellow researchers.

The Computational Pivot

The COVID-19 lockdown became a catalyst for growth.

I immersed myself in the world of data science, machine learning, and programming, recognizing it as the future of medical discovery.

My passion for analyzing complex data led me to computational neuroscience and machine learning. I immersed myself in learning programming (R, Python) and applying advanced AI and statistical modeling techniques, particularly for analyzing neurophysiological (EEG/MEG) and neuroimaging (MRI) data. My postdoctoral research allowed me to delve deeper into this area, investigating brain dynamics and aging using these methods.

Phase 2: Mastering Computational & Data Science

  • Research Fellowship in applying ML/DL to neuroimaging (MRI) and electrophysiological (EEG/MEG) data.
  • Expertise in complex data analysis, biostatistics and computational methods.
  • Developed and published open-source R packages for clinical database extraction and analysis.

My first major project ~ 3 year.

  • Developed a deep learning model and deployed a public ML-powered risk calculator.

A defining aspect of my research path has been managing and collaborating on large-scale projects. I initiated and spearheaded this significant international collaboration during the COVID-19 pandemic. This experience honed my skills in project management, data coordination, and collaborative science.

Phase 3: Leadership & AI-Driven Innovation

  • Leading the **Shaheen Clinic Research Group** and serving in leadership roles in global consortia.
  • Developing novel LLM-based frameworks to **automate systematic reviews** and clinical trial analysis.
  • Building practical tools: Deep Learning MRI segmentation tools, BioMedical Pathway Engine, and R/Python packages.
My commitment to contributing to the scientific community is reflected in my extensive publication record of over 60+ works and an H-index of 15. I actively engage in peer review and hold editorial positions, giving me insight into the publication process. I also enjoy translating complex methods into practical tools, developing open-source packages and AI applications for researchers.

Scaling Knowledge: Shaheen Clinic LLC

A U.S. Company to Empower Researchers Globally

To formalize and expand my commitment to education, I founded Shaheen Clinic LLC.

  • Provides advanced training in medical research and data science.
  • Develops and distributes practical tools for data analysis.
  • Offers expert research consultations for academics and institutions.

Visit Shaheen Clinic LLC

To make a broader impact, I realized I needed to scale my efforts in teaching and tool development. That's why I established Shaheen Clinic as a U.S. company. It's a platform to formally offer the training, tools, and consulting that I am passionate about, helping to bridge the gap between clinical knowledge and cutting-edge computational skills for researchers around the world.
